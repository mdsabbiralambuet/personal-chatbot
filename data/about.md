# About Sabbir

I'm a graduate student at the University of Pittsburgh studying engineering,
with research interests at the intersection of MEMS/microfabrication and
soft-material mechanics.

# Current Research

My main project is developing flexible piezoresistive and capacitive pressure
sensors using microstructured PDMS. The geometry of the microstructures
(microdomes, micropyramids) dramatically improves sensitivity at low pressures.
The fabrication pipeline includes silicon mold etching with KOH or TMAH,
photoresist reflow for curved features, PDMS casting, and conductive coatings
like sprayed CNT, PEDOT:PSS, or AgNW.

# Beyond Pressure Sensing

I'm interested in moving from scalar pressure to full 3D stress decoupling —
sensors that can resolve normal and shear components of contact force
independently, useful for robotic tactile sensing and prosthetic skin.

# Hobby Projects

Outside research, I build hardware projects with Arduino and write physics-based
browser visualizations — soft-material tribology animations, stress heatmaps,
that kind of thing.
